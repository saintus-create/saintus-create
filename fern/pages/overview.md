The Encyclopedia of Rhetoric covers rhetorical terms, figures of speech, historical movements, and major theorists from ancient Syracuse to the information age.

## Two Ways to Browse

You can navigate the encyclopedia in two complementary ways:

- **Alphabetically** — scan entries letter by letter, exactly as you would in a printed reference volume. Every entry from *Accumulatio* to *Young, Richard* is accessible through the sidebar or the dedicated alphabetical index.
- **Thematically** — jump straight to a category that matches your interest: the figures of speech that shape eloquence, the historical eras that defined rhetorical practice, or the individual theorists whose works anchor the discipline.

<Tip>
  If you already know the term or name you're looking for, the fastest route is the **search bar** at the top of the page. Full-text search scans titles, descriptions, and body text across entries simultaneously.
</Tip>

## About the Collection

Entries take four forms — brief **identifications**, elaborated **notes**, in-depth **essays**, and full **articles** — so the depth of treatment scales with the importance and complexity of each topic. Every entry includes a definition and discussion; most include a bibliography and cross-references to related articles.

<Note>
  The encyclopedia spans 23 letter sections. Letters **J** and **X** currently have no entries in this edition.
</Note>
