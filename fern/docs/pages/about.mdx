This one-volume reference work provides an introduction to rhetoric, including the major periods and personages, concepts and applications. Rhetoric, though the oldest and broadest of the humanities, draws on disciplines like anthropology, linguistics, philosophy, and psychology.

The Encyclopedia's entries take four forms:

- **Identifications** — brief definitions of a figure, term, or concept
- **Notes** — elaborated explanations of key topics
- **Essays** — in-depth explorations of major subjects
- **Articles** — full treatments illuminating rhetoric's art and methodology

## How to Use This Reference

Browse alphabetically using the sidebar, or use the search bar to find specific terms, figures, or concepts. Each entry includes a definition, discussion, and where available, a bibliography and cross-references to related entries.

## Editor's Note

Confining the spaciousness of rhetoric and its 2,500-year history of theory and praxis into one volume cannot completely capture rhetoric's metadisciplinary nature. This Encyclopedia can suggest the scope of rhetoric as it is studied in different disciplines in American higher education and give future direction in communicating within and across complex, multilayered discourse communities.
